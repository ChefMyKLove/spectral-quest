2. Project Description (500 words max)

Project Overview:

This project creates original visual artworks establishing the aesthetic universe of "Spectral Quest: Rainbow Craft," a queer-led fantasy transmedia project featuring Lirien, a non-binary unicorn weaver, and their journey to save their cousin Maxim from transformation into a weapon of spectral desaturation. As a Nanaimo-based queer visual artist, I will develop comprehensive concept art, character designs, and environmental paintings that center 2SLGBTQI+ narratives and introduce powerful queer protagonists into the fantasy genre.

The Story: Ten years ago, High Cleric Voss weaponized the Spectral Core against two young unicorn weavers‚ÄîLirien and their cousin Maxim‚Äîwhose collaborative rainbow-weaving threatened his vision of binary purity. Maxim's silver horn was severed, and over ten years of torture, they were transformed into a cyborg processor powering the Desaturation Engine. Lirien, burned and exiled, was raised by seven rebel Dancers in the Crimson Aether. Now 20 years old, Lirien must journey through seven fractured rainbow layers, collecting 159 Spectral Motes to restore the Harmony Shards and save Maxim from the machine that consumed them.

Visual Art Focus: This intensive 8-week project will produce standalone visual artworks including:

  • Character Designs (15 total): Lirien (non-binary protagonist), Maxim/Maximum (transformation from dancer to cyborg), the Seven Dancers (Ember, Calen, Sol, Sylvara, Zorah, Nyx, Veyra), High Cleric Voss, supporting characters
  • Environmental Concepts (7 layers): Each rainbow layer with distinct aesthetic‚ÄîCrimson Aether, Amber Flux, Solar Rift, Verdant Pulse, Azure Void, Indigo Veil, Violet Crown
  • Key Scenes (8 illustrations): The Shattering, The Seven Dancers mentoring Lirien, Lirien riding rainbow carpets, the Desaturation Engine, pivotal prophecy moments
  • Promotional Materials: 6 printable posters (various sizes), social media template sets

Artistic Approach: The visual style blends traditional hand-sketching with digital illustration, emphasizing rainbow gradients, emotional depth through color theory, and non-binary aesthetics that reject rigid binaries. Using traditional media (inks, paints) and digital tools (Photoshop, Procreate), I will create layered artworks exploring themes of identity, resilience, and queer joy.

Cultural Significance: This project addresses severe underrepresentation of queer characters in gaming (only 2% feature LGBTQ+ leads, per GLAAD) and fantasy literature. By centering Lirien‚Äîa non-binary unicorn whose queerness is integral to their power‚Äîthis work introduces a new queer icon into popular culture. Visual outputs will engage Vancouver Island queer communities through partnerships with QMUNITY, Generation Q, and VI Queer Collective.

Community Impact: Free beta testing with local queer youth, Pride Month 2026 launch events, and ongoing workshops on digital storytelling will directly benefit underserved Island communities. Visual art created during this intensive will serve as the foundation for a larger transmedia project (game and novels), with 10% of eventual proceeds supporting Vancouver Island LGBTQ2SIA+ youth programs.

9. Pro Tips for Success

From Successful BC Arts Council Recipients:

  1. Start NOW: Don't wait. Letters of support take 2-3 weeks. Begin immediately.
  2. Be specific: "Create character designs" is weak. "Create 15 full-body character designs with 3 pose variations each" is strong.
  3. Show community ties: Reviewers want to see you're embedded in your community, not isolated. Partnerships matter.
  4. Equity is strength: Don't downplay your 2SLGBTQI+ identity or rural location‚Äîthese are advantages in equity-focused funding.
  5. Quality over quantity in portfolio: 5 exceptional pieces beat 10 mediocre ones. Curate ruthlessly.
  6. Budget honesty: Don't inflate costs (reviewers know prices). Don't underbid yourself either (they'll question if you can deliver).
  7. Follow instructions exactly: File formats, page limits, naming conventions‚Äîfollow them to the letter.
  8. Proofread obsessively: Typos signal carelessness. Have 2-3 people review before submitting.
  9. If you don't get it: Request feedback and apply again. Persistence pays off.
  10. Celebrate applying: Win or lose, completing a grant application is professional development. You've grown.

13. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Do These Things:

  • Don't submit generic letters of support: "We support this artist" is worthless. Needs specifics: "We will provide space for 3 feedback sessions with 15 queer youth ages 16-25"
  • Don't use jargon without explanation: "BSV blockchain" means nothing to most reviewers. Say "blockchain technology that enables micropayments and digital collectibles"
  • Don't apologize for being emerging: "I know my portfolio isn't very strong but..." = instant rejection. Own your emerging status as fresh perspective.
  • Don't ignore the word limits: If they say 500 words, submit 498-502 words. Not 750. Not 300.
  • Don't budget for things you can't explain: Every line item should have clear justification. "Misc supplies $500" won't fly.
  • Don't forget to say "I": This is YOUR application. "The artist will create..." is weaker than "I will create..."
  • Don't submit at 11:58 PM on deadline: Aim for 2 days early. System crashes happen. Murphy's Law is real.
  • Don't include everything you've ever made: Curate ruthlessly. Quality > quantity always.
  • Don't write in pure academic language: Reviewers are humans. Write clearly, passionately, but professionally.
  • Don't give up if rejected: First-time success rate is ~25%. Persistence matters. Request feedback and reapply.
