2. Project Description

Overview (500 words max): This project creates original visual artworks to establish the aesthetic universe of "Spectral Quest: Rainbow Prophecy," a queer-led fantasy video game and novel series launching in Pride Month 2026. As a Nanaimo-based queer visual artist, I will develop concept art for characters (e.g., cyborg-unicorns embodying non-binary resilience), scenes (rainbow realms fractured by desaturation), and promotional materials (posters, social templates) that affirm 2SLGBTQI+ identities. These visuals will prototype merch like stuffed unicorns and action figures, transforming digital sketches into tangible queer icons.

The core artistic focus is on rainbow-gradient illustrations using digital and traditional media, exploring themes of prophecy, identity, and joy amid erasure. Outputs include 20+ character/scene sketches, 10 printable posters, 5 social media template sets, and 3D prototypes for merch (e.g., unicorn stuffies via Custom Prototypes). This aligns with BC Arts Council's equity priorities by centering underrepresented queer narratives in visual arts, fostering community through Island-based creation and Vancouver Pride outreach.

While tied to a broader media project (game dev funded separately via Media Arts Grants), this application isolates visual creation as fine art: hand-sketched inks evolving into digital renders, printed as collectible posters. The 8-week intensive will yield a cohesive art book/PDF for queer gamers, amplifying representation (only 2% of games feature queer leads, per GLAAD). Impact: Engage 500+ via local betas; donate 10% merch proceeds to QMUNITY.

Artistic Goals:

  • Develop 10 character designs (e.g., Lirien the non-binary unicorn) with layered inks/paints for emotional depth.
  • Create 5 scene illustrations for novel covers/prophecy lore.
  • Prototype merch visuals: 2 stuffie patterns, 2 action fig sculpts, poster layouts.
  • Produce promotional assets: Editable Canva templates for X/Instagram (#QueerGaming).

Rationale for Visual Arts Fit: Though game/novel-adjacent, this emphasizes standalone visual outputs (sketches, prints, prototypes) as contemporary craft, akin to graphic novels or digital fine art exhibitions.
