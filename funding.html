7. Grant Application Best Practices

Before You Apply:

  • Read Guidelines Thoroughly: Every grant has specific eligibility requirements, deadlines, and restrictions. Don't waste time on grants you're not eligible for.
  • Tailor Each Application: Use these templates as starting points but customize extensively for each grant's priorities and language.
  • Build Your Portfolio: Have ready: playable game demo/prototype, art samples (character designs, level concepts), novel excerpt (30-50 pages), CV highlighting relevant experience, letters of support from community partners.
  • Budget Realistically: Research actual costs (software subscriptions, contractor rates, printing costs). Use specific numbers with sources.
  • Emphasize Impact: Grants fund impact, not just ideas. Show how your project serves underserved communities, addresses representation gaps, creates opportunities.
  • Get Feedback: Have 2-3 trusted people review applications before submitting‚Äîideally someone familiar with grant writing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

  • Submitting generic "copy-paste" applications
  • Focusing only on what YOU get, not community benefit
  • Unclear or vague budget items
  • Missing deadlines (set reminders 2 weeks before)
  • Not following formatting requirements (page limits, font size, file types)
  • Overpromising ("This will change the world") without concrete metrics
  • Ignoring equity/diversity questions (if you're not from an equity-seeking group, be honest about how you'll center those voices)

After Submission:

  • Follow Up: If you haven't heard back within stated timeline, politely email to confirm receipt
  • Learn from Rejections: Many grants provide feedback. Request it and use it to strengthen future applications
  • Build Relationships: Connect with program officers, attend info sessions, join grant-winner networks
  • Apply Widely: Don't put all eggs in one basket. Apply to 5-10 grants simultaneously
  • Document Everything: Keep records of all applications, responses, and feedback for future reference
